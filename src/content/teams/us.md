## Ownership History

The US team was acquired in 2023 by a group led by Marc Lasry (Avenue Capital Group, co-founder) and the Avenue Sports Fund. At ~$35M, this was the largest team transaction in SailGP history at that point — a benchmark that has since been eclipsed by the $60M+ Denmark deal in 2026.

The ownership group was assembled with deliberate diversity: Issa Rae (actress/writer/producer), Deontay Wilder (boxing heavyweight champion), and Jozy Altidore (Major League Soccer) joined Lasry as celebrity co-investors. Russell Coutts explicitly described this composition as strategic — celebrity owners bring media coverage and cultural reach that money alone cannot replicate.

## Strategic Significance

The Lasry acquisition was a proof-of-concept for the private ownership model. Prior to 2023, SailGP teams were largely league-owned or held by sailing insiders. Lasry's entry signaled that mainstream sports investors saw SailGP as a credible asset class.

The diverse celebrity group also signaled an intentional effort to broaden SailGP's demographic appeal beyond its historical white, affluent, coastal sailing audience.

## Commercial Status

No title sponsor is currently named in public sources as of early 2026. This is notable given the team's high-profile ownership group — it suggests either an undisclosed arrangement or an active commercial search.

## Competitive Notes

The US team has been competitive in the racing but has not won the championship. The US market is critical for broadcast growth (CBS deal, potential future rights expansion), making commercial performance of this team a league priority.
