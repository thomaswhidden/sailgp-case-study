## Ownership

Mubadala Investment Company is one of Abu Dhabi's two sovereign wealth funds (alongside ADIA). It manages approximately $300B AUM and has significant sports investments globally (McLaren Racing, Manchester City-related properties, FC Barcelona stake discussions).

Mubadala's acquisition of the Brazil team is unusual for several reasons:
1. A Gulf sovereign wealth fund owning a South American team is an unusual geographic pairing — it suggests Mubadala sees SailGP as a global brand-building vehicle, not primarily a local market play.
2. Mubadala also has a commercial relationship with SailGP through the Abu Dhabi race venue (where the record 3.47M US viewership figure was set in November 2025).
3. The dual role as both team owner and host city commercial partner creates a potential governance/conflict-of-interest question.

## Strategic Notes

Brazil entering in 2025 made SailGP the first global sailing league with a South American team. The Brazilian market has a large, passionate sports audience, and the association with a high-prestige global league could help develop sailing's profile domestically.

The Mubadala-branded team is also commercially unusual — usually the title sponsor is a consumer brand, not the ownership entity itself. This suggests Mubadala's primary goal is institutional brand building rather than team-level commercial development.
