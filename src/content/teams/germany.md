## Ownership

Germany has assembled a notable ownership group:
- **Sebastian Vettel** — 4× Formula 1 World Champion; the most prominent F1 driver with strong sustainability credentials (he has been vocally environmental). His SailGP investment fits his post-retirement positioning.
- **David Blitzer** — Harris Blitzer Sports & Entertainment owns the New Jersey Devils (NHL), Crystal Palace FC (Premier League), and stakes in multiple other teams. A sophisticated multi-sport institutional investor.
- **Joe Tsai** — Co-founder of Alibaba, owner of Brooklyn Nets (NBA). Tsai's involvement signals SailGP's Asia strategy (Tsai has deep China connections) as much as it signals financial credibility.
- **Deutsche Bank** — Germany's largest bank as a presenting partner (not equity owner) is a significant sponsorship win for the German domestic market.

## Competitive Notes

Germany was the only team not to have won a race as of the TIME January 2024 interview. This is a commercial challenge: sponsors need competitive success to justify team-level investment. The team's racing performance relative to its ownership caliber is a notable discrepancy.

## Strategic Significance

The Vettel / Tsai combination bridges SailGP's F1 comparisons (Vettel is F1 royalty) and its Asia expansion ambitions (Tsai's Chinese network). This team's ownership is arguably the most strategically layered in the league.
