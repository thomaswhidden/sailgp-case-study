Per Andrew Thompson's August 2025 disclosure, the Giles Scott move represents the first formal transfer fee paid between two sailing teams in the sport's history. The transfer fee counted toward the Canada team's $10M cost cap.

This event marks a structural professionalization milestone for sailing as a sport, paralleling the development of transfer markets in football, basketball, and other professional team sports. SailGP is actively monitoring transfer market dynamics — Thompson explicitly noted concern about salary inflation as 5-6 elite sailors become attractive to multiple teams.

Five-year contracts have begun appearing (notably at the Italian team), suggesting teams are using contract length as a tool to lock in talent before the market matures further.
