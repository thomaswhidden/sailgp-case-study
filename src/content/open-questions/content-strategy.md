# Content strategy beyond the docuseries

## What's known

- "Uncharted" docuseries launched July 2025 (after Thompson interview)
- Thompson teased "a couple more ideas up our sleeve" beyond Uncharted
- Existing series "Racing on the Edge" (Rolex-funded) distributes via broadcast partners and YouTube
- Thompson is skeptical that copying Drive to Survive is enough: "the market is moving on a little bit from that now... what's the next thing for content?"
- TikTok and YouTube have driven the most measurable audience growth, with one viral clip hitting ~50M views

## What's not known

What the next-generation content strategy looks like and how it integrates with the broadcast/streaming model.

## Questions to ask Coutts

1. Beyond Uncharted, what's the next major content initiative SailGP is investing in?
2. Thompson said "the market is moving on" from Drive to Survive — what's your read on what content format actually moves the needle for an emerging sport in 2026 and beyond?
3. Is SailGP planning to build out direct-to-fan content (a SailGP+ streaming product, for example) or stay distribution-partner-dependent?
4. How are you thinking about creator partnerships — bringing TikTok/YouTube native talent into SailGP storytelling vs. building athlete-led channels?
5. What's the budget envelope for content — is content still a marketing line item, or is it becoming a profit-and-loss center on its own?
