# Cost cap evolution and the marketing exception

## What's known

Thompson (Aug 2025): The current team cost cap is **$10M USD covering everything** — sailor salaries, accommodation, travel, training, business support, commercial, marketing, boats, logistics. SailGP is **actively in discussion with teams** to remove certain categories (notably marketing and social media spend) from the cap so teams can invest more in fan engagement.

## What's not known

The new effective cap structure and what stays inside the cap envelope.

## Questions to ask Coutts

1. Is the marketing exception decided, and if so, what's the new effective cap?
2. What other categories are under discussion for exclusion? Athlete-development pipeline spending? Hospitality investment?
3. Is there a maximum that teams can spend on any single excluded category, or is it uncapped?
4. What's the governance mechanism — do the teams collectively vote on cap changes, or is this a league-level decision?
5. How does SailGP's cost cap structure compare to F1's cost cap (which is $135M USD currently and excludes driver salaries, marketing, and other categories)?
6. As franchise valuations rise toward $100M+, will the cost cap rise proportionally to give teams room to compete commercially?
