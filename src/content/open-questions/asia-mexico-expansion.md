# Asian and Mexican market expansion

## What's known

Thompson (Aug 2025) explicitly named China, Japan, Mexico, and additional South American markets as expansion targets. SailGP's growth pace philosophy: deliberately one or two teams per year, one venue per year. Not a fire sale.

The 20-team target (2 pools of 10) suggests room for 7+ more teams beyond the current 13 (post-Sweden/Artemis joining for Season 6).

## What's not known

The specific timing, pricing, and regulatory/political plan for these markets — particularly China, which raises distinct geopolitical risk questions.

## Questions to ask Coutts

1. What's the realistic timeline for adding teams in China, Japan, and Mexico? Season 7? Season 8?
2. Are these team-additions or venue-additions first? (i.e., does SailGP race in Tokyo before there's a Japan team?)
3. What does franchise pricing look like for a new market? Is the $50M floor still the entry point, or is it different for strategic markets?
4. China specifically: how do you think about geopolitical risk and the regulatory environment? Has the conversation with potential Chinese partners begun?
5. Is there a model where SailGP partners with a national federation or sovereign wealth-style backer for a market, vs. requiring a fully private team owner?
6. How are you balancing the growth pace philosophy against the commercial pull of these markets — could you go faster than 1–2 teams/year if you wanted to?
