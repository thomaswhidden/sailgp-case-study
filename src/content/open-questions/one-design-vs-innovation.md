- The F50 is a one-design platform. Formula 1 allows (and requires) teams to build their own cars — and the engineering arms race is a major part of F1's appeal. When, if ever, does SailGP consider moving off a pure one-design format?
- How does Coutts weigh the cost-control argument for one-design (lower barriers to entry, no rich-team advantage) against the innovation appeal of open classes (engineering narrative, IP development, manufacturer interest)?
- Is SailGP Technologies (Southampton) working on a next-generation boat? What is the design philosophy — evolution of the F50, or something fundamentally different?
- Could SailGP attract major marine manufacturers (Prada, BMW Marine, LVMH-backed brands) as technical partners if there were more open development?
