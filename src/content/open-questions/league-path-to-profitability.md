# League-level path to profitability

## What's known

Thompson disclosed (Aug 2025) that **three or four teams will hit at least break-even this season**. Top teams generate $8–9M in revenue against a $10M cost cap. Teams are sustainable.

## What's not known

The **league itself** — separate from team economics — has never disclosed P&L. Ellison has personally underwritten capital costs since 2018. Thompson confirmed the model has shifted from "owners covering team costs" to genuine franchise value, but did not address whether SailGP at the league level (central operations, boat manufacturing, broadcast production, logistics, marketing) has reached operating breakeven without Ellison's funding.

## Questions to ask Coutts

1. At the league level, has SailGP reached operating breakeven? If not, what's the timeline?
2. What proportion of league operating costs are still being underwritten by Ellison vs. covered by team fees, sponsorship, broadcast, and event revenue?
3. As broadcast deals mature in the next 5 years, what does the revenue mix look like at maturity? (Today it's predominantly sponsorship.)
4. What's the planned trajectory for the central cost base as the league scales from 12 to 20 teams?
