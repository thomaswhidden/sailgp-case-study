# Gaming & betting as a revenue stream

## What's known

Thompson (Aug 2025): SailGP has commissioned studies showing the sport lends itself well to betting because the one-design boats create "level playing field" dynamics similar to handicap horse racing — anyone can win. SailGP has an internal team generating odds. **Bet365 has already taken SailGP odds** as a live product.

Thompson explicitly framed this as "something that gets people more of a reason to get into the sport and care about the sport."

## What's not known

The commercial structure of betting integrations and the league's revenue capture model.

## Questions to ask Coutts

1. Is there an exclusive sportsbook partnership in the pipeline (the way F1 and the major US leagues have done with FanDuel, DraftKings, etc.)?
2. What's the league's revenue capture model from betting — fixed rights fee, revenue share on handle, or both?
3. Are there jurisdictional regulatory concerns given SailGP's global venue footprint (US, UK, Australia, NZ, France, Italy, UAE, etc.)?
4. Will there be in-app or in-broadcast betting integration, the way F1 has experimented with?
5. How does SailGP think about integrity and match-fixing risk — what governance structure is in place to prevent insider information from athletes or central staff being misused?
6. Is fantasy SailGP a planned product?
