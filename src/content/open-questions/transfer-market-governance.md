# Transfer market governance as it matures

## What's known

Thompson (Aug 2025): The nationality rule was changed from one to three sailors required from the affiliated nation, opening a transfer market. **First-ever sailing transfer fee:** Giles Scott from Britain to Canada (Scott also took ownership equity in Canada). Transfer fees count toward the $10M team cost cap. Italian team has signed athletes to 5-year contracts.

## What's not known

The transfer market is brand new — Thompson explicitly called it "a start of something to come." The governance infrastructure that exists in mature sports (salary floor, separate transfer fee accounting, agent regulations, escrow rules, contract registration body, dispute resolution) has not been described.

## Questions to ask Coutts

1. As the transfer market matures, will there be a salary floor as well as a cap?
2. Will transfer fees be accounted separately from operating costs (the way they are in football), or remain inside the $10M team cap?
3. Is there a planned league-level athlete contract registry?
4. How will agent representation be regulated — or is that out of scope for the league?
5. What's the dispute resolution mechanism between teams over contested transfers?
6. At what point does SailGP need a CBA-style athlete agreement, given the increasing professionalization of the sailor pool?
