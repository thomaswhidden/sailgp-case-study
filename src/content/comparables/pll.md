## Profile

The Premier Lacrosse League was founded in 2018 by brothers Paul and Mike Rabil. Paul Rabil was the sport's biggest star and co-founded the league specifically to disrupt Major League Lacrosse (MLL), an older league that the Rabils believed had commercial and structural limitations.

**Format:** 8 teams, touring model (events at college venues), ~14 events per season.

**Business model:** Started as league-owned teams (similar to SailGP's early model), transitioning to franchise ownership with Bowlcamp Capital (a sports-focused PE fund) taking a controlling stake.

## Lessons for SailGP

1. **The founder-to-franchise transition is hard.** PLL's early seasons were marked by operational challenges as the league moved from league control to owner-operator dynamics. SailGP is navigating the same transition.
2. **Niche sports can grow audiences but slowly.** PLL's viewership and attendance have grown, but it remains a niche property despite lacrosse's genuine growth at the youth and collegiate level.
3. **PE entry is a milestone, not a destination.** Bowlcamp's investment in PLL gave the league financial stability, but commercial growth has been gradual.
4. **The touring model vs. home market model is a structural choice.** PLL uses a touring model (events at neutral venues) like early SailGP. Building home-market fan bases is a different and harder challenge.

## Key Contrasts

- **Sport:** Lacrosse has a larger youth participation base in the US than sailing.
- **Scale:** PLL is smaller commercially than SailGP.
- **Global:** SailGP is explicitly global; PLL is primarily North American.
