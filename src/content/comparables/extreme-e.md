## Profile

**Extreme E** is an off-road electric SUV racing series founded by Alejandro Agag (Formula E co-founder), racing in remote and environmentally significant locations (Amazon, Arctic, Himalayas) with a gender-equal lineup requirement. It emphasizes the environmental damage visible at race sites as part of its messaging.

**E1 Series** is an electric powerboat racing series (launched 2023) that is even more directly comparable to SailGP — racing in harbor/city venues, electric propulsion, global calendar, team-based format.

## Extreme E's Challenges

- **Low viewership:** Racing in remote locations means spectators cannot attend. TV and streaming numbers have been modest.
- **Production costs:** Staging races in the Amazon or Arctic is extremely expensive relative to the audience reached.
- **Unclear commercial model:** Broadcast rights, sponsorship, and franchise economics have not been publicly disclosed, and the league has reportedly faced financial difficulties.
- **Celebrity ownership:** Teams owned by Lewis Hamilton, Nico Rosberg, Jenson Button, Nico Hülkenberg — racing pedigree but smaller commercial reach than SailGP's celebrity base.

## E1 Series

E1 races electric powerboats in city harbors, which is the closest format to SailGP's product positioning. Teams include Rafa Nadal, Diddy (prior to his legal issues), and others. The series is in early commercial development and has not yet established a significant audience.

## Lessons for SailGP

1. **Remote venues are a liability, not an asset.** Spectacular as Extreme E's locations are, the inability for spectators to attend caps the product's commercial value. SailGP's harbor-front positioning is strategically superior.
2. **The sustainability narrative alone is insufficient.** Extreme E's entire premise is environmental messaging, but it hasn't generated mainstream sports audiences. SailGP pairs sustainability with genuine racing quality and stadium-sport experience.
3. **Electric propulsion is coming.** E1's bet on electric powerboats may eventually be right. If electric propulsion becomes viable for foiling boats, SailGP could face direct competition from a more technologically current format.
4. **Category crowding.** Multiple "sustainability + global racing" leagues create category confusion for sponsors. SailGP needs to differentiate clearly.

## Key Contrasts

- **Spectator access:** SailGP at city harbors vs. Extreme E in remote locations.
- **Commercial stage:** SailGP at $200M revenue vs. Extreme E's undisclosed (likely much smaller) revenue.
- **Sailing heritage:** SailGP has genuine world-class sailing; Extreme E is a new category with limited sporting legacy.
