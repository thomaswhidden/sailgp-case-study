## Profile

Formula 1 is the world's premier single-seater motorsport championship, founded in 1950. Liberty Media acquired commercial rights in 2017 for $4.4 billion and has since transformed the property from a niche motorsport into a mainstream global entertainment brand.

**Revenue (2024):** ~$3.6 billion total, including ~$750M in broadcast rights, ~$800M in race promotion fees, ~$650M in sponsorship, and ~$1.3B in team fees and other commercial income.

**Teams:** 10 teams, all private, with franchise values ranging from ~$200M (smaller teams) to $10B+ (Ferrari, Mercedes, Red Bull).

## The Liberty Media Transformation

Before Liberty Media, F1 was controlled by Bernie Ecclestone and CVC Capital Partners. The property was:
- Broadcast-hostile: Ecclestone optimized for live gate revenue, not TV
- Data-dark: minimal analytics, no official social media strategy
- Fan-unfriendly: high ticket prices, poor fan experience

Liberty Media's transformation:
- **Drive to Survive** (Netflix, 2019–): Created a casual fan acquisition machine that has driven F1's US audience from ~500K to 3M+ per race.
- **Social media opening:** F1 became one of the most-engaged sporting brands on TikTok and Instagram.
- **US market investment:** Added Austin (2012 under Ecclestone), Miami (2022), Las Vegas (2023) as marquee US stops.
- **Broadcast renegotiation:** Rights values have risen substantially at renewal.

## Lessons for SailGP

1. **The docuseries playbook works.** Drive to Survive is the proof that casual sports audiences can be captured via narrative content, not just live racing. SailGP's "Uncharted" is a direct attempt to replicate this.
2. **US market is the key unlock.** F1's commercial inflection correlated with US market growth. SailGP's CBS deal and record US viewership figures suggest a similar trajectory is beginning.
3. **Franchise value acceleration.** F1 franchise values grew 10–20× under Liberty Media in seven years. SailGP's 70%+ team valuation growth in three years is on a similar trajectory but from a much lower base.
4. **Per-race economics gap.** F1 earns ~$200–400M per race in direct and indirect commercial value. SailGP's $26M regional economic impact per event is real but in a different order of magnitude.

## Key Contrasts

- **Governance:** F1 is a constructors' championship — teams build their own cars. SailGP is one-design.
- **Scale:** F1's 24 races across 20 countries; SailGP at 12 events is far smaller.
- **History:** F1 has 75 years of accumulated brand equity; SailGP is 7 years old.
- **Sport:** Motorsport has a broader global audience ceiling than sailing.
