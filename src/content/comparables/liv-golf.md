## Profile

LIV Golf launched in 2022 as a Saudi Arabia Public Investment Fund-backed alternative golf league, signing top PGA Tour players with guaranteed contracts worth hundreds of millions of dollars. The league runs 14 events with 13 teams in a team-and-individual format.

**Funding:** PIF has reportedly committed $2B+ to LIV Golf. It is not commercially self-sustaining — the league exists as a geopolitical project (sportswashing) as much as a commercial sports venture.

**Broadcast:** LIV Golf's broadcast strategy has been its most significant commercial failure. The PGA Tour's control of US broadcast relationships locked LIV out of traditional sports media. LIV eventually struck a deal with CW Network (secondary US broadcaster), then moved to streaming. Viewership has been low.

**PGA Tour merger talks:** In June 2023, PIF and the PGA Tour announced a framework agreement to merge commercial operations. As of 2025, this has not been consummated due to regulatory and governance complications.

## Lessons for SailGP

1. **Broadcast is survival.** LIV failed to secure meaningful broadcast distribution, and without it, sponsors cannot justify investment and casual fan acquisition is impossible. SailGP's CBS deal and growing US viewership distinguish it from LIV's broadcast isolation.
2. **Sovereign wealth as owner creates geopolitical exposure.** PIF's backing of LIV became a significant reputational liability. SailGP has Mubadala as a team owner (not league owner), which is a different risk profile — but the Middle East racing geography creates the same scrutiny.
3. **Player controversy is a double-edged sword.** LIV's player signings generated massive media coverage but much of it was negative. SailGP's celebrity ownership strategy generates coverage that is predominantly positive.
4. **Disruption requires commercial sustainability.** LIV has the financial resources to survive indefinitely on PIF's capital, but it is not building a sustainable commercial business. SailGP must be self-sustaining — which creates more discipline but also more risk.

## Key Contrasts

- **Funding model:** PIF subsidizes LIV indefinitely; SailGP is commercially driven (with Ellison backstop).
- **Incumbent relationship:** LIV declared war on the PGA Tour; SailGP's relationship with the America's Cup is more complex.
- **Broadcast:** LIV has poor distribution; SailGP has growing US viewership.
- **Sustainability narrative:** LIV's Saudi backing is the antithesis of SailGP's "Powered by Nature" positioning.
