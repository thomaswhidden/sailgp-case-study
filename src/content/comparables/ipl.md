## Profile

The Indian Premier League (Twenty20 cricket) was founded in 2008 by the BCCI and has become one of the world's most commercially valuable sporting leagues in under 20 years.

**Media rights:** The 2022 IPL media rights auction raised $6.2 billion for a 5-year cycle — one of the most valuable sports media rights deals in history, surpassing the NFL on a per-match basis.

**Franchise values:** IPL franchises are now valued at $1–2B each. The Mumbai Indians were purchased for $111M in 2008 and are now worth approximately $1.7B — a 15× appreciation in 16 years.

**Format:** 10 teams, 74 matches per season (round-robin + playoffs), 2-month season (March–May) in India.

## Private Equity Entry

The IPL pioneered the PE entry into cricket franchise ownership. Entities including CVC Capital Partners, RPSG Group, and major Indian conglomerates own franchises. The 2022 expansion (from 8 to 10 teams) generated ~$800M in expansion fees.

## Lessons for SailGP

1. **Emerging market + right format = franchise value explosion.** India's mobile internet penetration + cricket obsession + T20 format created a confluence that is almost unreplicable, but the lesson is that the right sport in the right market at the right time can move very fast.
2. **PE sees the pattern.** Ares Management's entry into SailGP (France team) and Avenue Sports Fund (US team) follow the same logic that drove PE into IPL: early-stage franchise ownership in a growing league creates asymmetric upside.
3. **Broadcaster competition drives value.** The IPL's 2022 auction had Disney Star and Viacom18 bidding against each other. SailGP needs to reach a scale where multiple broadcasters compete for rights.
4. **Celebrity ownership is a feature, not a bug.** Bollywood celebrity co-ownership of IPL teams (e.g., Shah Rukh Khan for Kolkata Knight Riders) pre-dates SailGP's strategy by 15 years and demonstrates that it works.

## Key Contrasts

- **Market size:** India's 1.4B people, predominantly cricket-mad, vs. SailGP's global-but-niche sailing audience.
- **Commercial maturity:** IPL is a $1.5B+ revenue property; SailGP is at $200M.
- **Governing body:** BCCI controls IPL as the national cricket board; SailGP is founder-controlled.
