## Strengths

- The F50 foiling catamaran is a visually spectacular product. At 60+ mph, within 200 meters of a harbor shoreline, the racing is legible to non-sailors in a way that offshore or ocean racing is not. This solves sailing's core TV problem: it is no longer "white triangles on a blue background."
- Stadium-style courses are short (under 30 minutes per race), which fits broadcast windows, social media clips, and spectator attention spans. Compare to America's Cup match-race formats that can last hours.
- The regular-season, multi-event format (12 events in Season 5; 13-event expansion planned) creates a championship narrative that broadcasts can follow across months — like F1 or Formula E. This is structurally absent from the America's Cup.
- Parity is improving. Australia won the first three championships; by 2024, all teams except Germany had won at least one race. Competitive parity matters commercially because it sustains multiple teams' sponsorship appeal.
- The Grand Prix final format, where top teams from the season compete in a winner-takes-all final, creates a stakes escalation. This mirrors F1's points-then-playoffs logic.

## Weaknesses / Risks

- One-design limits the engineering innovation narrative. F1's car development arms race is part of its appeal. SailGP's one-design philosophy controls costs but removes a significant engagement layer for technically oriented fans.
- Single boat class creates fragility. If SailGP Technologies (Southampton HQ) faces a production disruption, there is no alternative supplier. The entire league depends on a single manufacturer.
- Weather dependency creates scheduling uncertainty that is hard to manage in broadcast contracts. The Christchurch cancellation is an example of a format that has no rain-delay equivalent built in.
- 13 teams (2026) is approaching the outer limit of clean round-robin scheduling. Format complexity grows non-linearly as team count increases.

## Open Questions

- When, if ever, does SailGP move off the one-design F50 platform? Is there a next-generation boat in development? What is the design-development roadmap?
- How does Coutts balance the cost-control rationale for one-design with the innovation/excitement argument for open classes?
- What is the backup plan if F50 production is disrupted? Is there a spare fleet? A secondary manufacturer?
- Has the league explored a two-tiered format (a lower division or feeder series) to deepen the competitive pyramid?
