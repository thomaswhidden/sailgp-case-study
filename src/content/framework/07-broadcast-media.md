## Strengths

- US CBS deal: the Spain 2023 race drew 1.78M viewers on CBS — the most-watched US sailing broadcast since 1992, beating F1 Brazil that same weekend.
- Abu Dhabi 2025 race: 3.47M US viewers — the most-watched sailing race in US history. This is a significant benchmark.
- "Uncharted" docuseries launched July 2025 (Apple TV+ / multiple broadcasters). This is the "Drive to Survive" moment that Coutts identified as critical for casual fan acquisition. Viewership data not yet public.
- SailGP's own YouTube channel generated ~2.1M views for its in-house series (as of the TIME interview, January 2024), demonstrating owned-media capability.
- Broadcast is available in 180+ countries, though depth of distribution (flagship broadcasters vs. minor digital sublicenses) is unclear.

## Weaknesses / Risks

- Broadcast rights values are not public. The CBS deal value is unknown. Compared to F1's ~$750M/year in broadcast rights or the NFL's multi-billion deals, SailGP is almost certainly at a much earlier stage.
- The CBS deal is primarily for US racing on CBS Sports Network (cable) rather than the main CBS broadcast network, which caps potential audience.
- No clear streaming-first strategy is articulated. Is SailGP building toward a direct-to-consumer streaming product, or is it dependent on broadcast partners?
- Docuseries performance ("Uncharted") is unproven. If it underperforms Drive to Survive comparables, the fan-acquisition catalyst thesis weakens.
- International broadcast deals appear fragmented — many markets are covered by secondary digital platforms rather than flagship sports broadcasters.

## Open Questions

- What is the path to a larger US broadcast deal — main CBS, ESPN, or a streaming-native deal (Netflix, Amazon)?
- What is the current broadcast rights revenue, and what is the target?
- Is there a direct-to-consumer streaming strategy? Would SailGP consider its own OTT product?
- How has "Uncharted" performed — viewership, social media impact, measurable fan acquisition effect?
- What is the broadcast rights deal structure — fixed fee, revenue share, barter (rights for production)?
