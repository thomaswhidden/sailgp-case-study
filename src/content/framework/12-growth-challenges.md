## Growth Challenges

**Talent pipeline depth:** F50 foiling is an extremely technical discipline. The global pool of sailors capable of performing at SailGP level is small and has not been formally estimated publicly. If growth to 20 teams requires 140+ top-level sailors (7 crew × 20 teams), and that pool doesn't exist yet, team quality degrades with expansion.

**Safety incidents:** The Auckland 2026 NZ–France collision sidelined both teams mid-season. High-speed foiling sailing has inherent collision risk. A serious athlete injury at a televised event would be reputationally catastrophic. SailGP's safety record so far is good, but the risk profile of F50 racing at 60 mph in close quarters is not trivial.

**Environmental tensions:** The Christchurch Hector's dolphin cancellation demonstrated that venues can be pulled by environmental protection enforcement with no warning. This creates force majeure risk in broadcast contracts and sponsor commitments. The "Powered by Nature" brand positioning makes environmental incidents especially damaging to narrative.

**Single-supplier F50 risk:** SailGP Technologies (Southampton) is the sole manufacturer of F50 boats. A fire, flood, production disruption, or financial failure at that facility could strand the entire league's racing season. There is no disclosed contingency for this scenario.

**Geopolitical exposure:** Middle East venues (Dubai, Abu Dhabi) expose SailGP to geopolitical risk that other leagues have struggled with (LIV Golf's Saudi controversy is the cautionary tale). Planned expansion to China adds sovereign-risk and US–China political tension exposure.

**Audience ceiling:** Sailing has a historically narrow demographic. The F1 comparison is aspirational but F1's audience is an order of magnitude larger with decades of investment behind it. If SailGP's audience plateaus at a mid-tier level, broadcast rights values will not scale to the levels needed to fully fund the league.

**Owner motivation alignment:** With PE (return-focused), sovereign wealth (geopolitical agenda), celebrity (brand building), and family office (long-term hold) owners, the diversity of motivation creates governance and commercial complexity as the league scales.

## Open Questions

- What is the operational change from the Auckland 2026 collision? Is there a review process underway?
- What is the contingency plan for an F50 production disruption?
- How does SailGP manage geopolitical risk in the Middle East, and what is the framework for evaluating expansion to China?
- Is there a formal safety review body, and what does it look at?
