## Strengths

- Iconic harbor venues: Sydney, San Francisco, Auckland, Bermuda, Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Taranto, Saint-Tropez, Cadiz, Hamilton Island — the venue list reads like a luxury travel catalog. This reinforces the premium brand positioning.
- $26.2 million average regional economic impact per event in Season 5 — four times the Season 1 average. This makes SailGP's pitch to host cities compelling on economic grounds.
- 112,000+ ticketed spectators in Season 5. 25,000 in Auckland; 20,000 in Portsmouth. These are meaningful live attendance numbers for a sailing event.
- Events are free to watch from the shore (with ticketed premium viewing options), which maximizes foot traffic and local media coverage while monetizing the premium segment.
- The geographic diversity of venues (Oceania, Europe, Americas, Middle East) supports the global league narrative and gives sponsors consistent activation opportunities across major markets.

## Weaknesses / Risks

- Venue instability: the Christchurch event was cancelled mid-event (March 2024) due to Hector's dolphins, a protected species. This is an extreme example of a venue risk that SailGP has no control over.
- St-Tropez was replaced by Geneva, suggesting not all venues are permanent. The cost and complexity of moving events is high.
- Host city economics favor SailGP (the event brings economic impact), but the structure of host fees, cost-sharing, and who bears production costs is not public. Are events profitable on a standalone basis for the league?
- Middle East venues (Dubai, Abu Dhabi) create the "sustainability vs. geography" tension prominently.
- The Auckland collision (February 2026) raised questions about course design safety in congested harbors.

## Open Questions

- What is the economic structure of a host city deal? What do cities pay in hosting fees, and what does SailGP pay in production costs?
- Are individual Grand Prix events profitable on a standalone basis (revenue from tickets, host fees, local sponsorship > production cost)?
- How are venues selected and rotated? Is there a competitive bid process like the Olympics, or is it Coutts's judgment?
- What is the plan for a US permanent venue — is there a US city that is being targeted as a flagship stop?
- How did the Christchurch cancellation get resolved financially and operationally? Did SailGP bear the cost, or the host?
