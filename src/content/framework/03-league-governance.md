## Strengths

- Strong founder-CEO alignment. Coutts is both co-founder and CEO, which reduces the principal–agent friction common in leagues where owners hire and fire commissioners. He has operational credibility with the sailing community and commercial credibility with investors.
- The one-design format centralizes equipment decisions at the league level, reducing the governance surface area. There is no arms-race over boat design, which simplifies the rules and avoids the owner lobbying dynamics seen in F1's Concorde Agreement negotiations.
- The league controls critical assets (F50 fleet, racing infrastructure) which gives it leverage over teams in a way that traditional franchised leagues (e.g., NFL) do not have.

## Weaknesses / Risks

- Governance structure is not public. Decision-making authority over season format, venue selection, team expansion, and broadcast rights is not transparent. How are owner voices incorporated? Is there a formal owners' council?
- Coutts's dual role as founder/CEO creates succession risk. There is no obvious #2 publicly visible. If Coutts departs, who runs SailGP?
- The Christchurch incident (March 2024) — where Coutts publicly criticized "minority interests" for the dolphin-related cancellation — revealed governance tension between the league's commercial interests and local regulatory/environmental authority. The tone of that public statement was arguably a governance failure.
- As ownership becomes more diverse (PE, sovereign wealth, celebrity, family office), governance complexity will increase. Misaligned owner objectives (e.g., Mubadala's geopolitical agenda vs. Ares's financial return vs. Reynolds's brand building) may produce conflict.

## Open Questions

- What is the formal governance structure? Is there a board? An owners' council with voting rights? What decisions are league-reserved vs. owner-reserved?
- How are owner conflicts resolved? Has there been a serious owner conflict the public record doesn't capture?
- What is Coutts's succession plan? Who is his deputy?
- How active vs. passive are the celebrity owners in operational decisions? Do Reynolds and Jackman have a governance role, or is it purely economic?
- What happens to governance if Ellison exits — does Coutts gain full control, or does a new capital structure emerge?
