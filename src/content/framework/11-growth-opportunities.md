## Growth Opportunities

**Team expansion to 20:** The stated league cap is 20 teams. From 13 in 2026, there are 7 expansion slots available. At $60M+ per franchise, that represents $420M+ in potential franchise fees — a significant source of capital for the league and existing owners.

**New market entry — China, Japan, Korea, India, Mexico:** These are the markets identified by Coutts. Each offers a major broadcast market, a wealthy ownership pool, and a growing sailing culture (particularly China and Japan, which have hosted America's Cup racing).

**Event count expansion:** Season 5 had 12 events; Coutts has referenced F1's 24-race calendar as aspirational. Going from 12 to 20+ events doubles the broadcast window, doubles sponsor activation, and amplifies the championship narrative.

**Broadcast rights growth:** If viewership continues to grow (3.47M Abu Dhabi viewers in 2025), broadcast rights will reprice at renewal. This is the single largest underdeveloped revenue line vs. comparable leagues.

**Licensing and merchandise:** Almost entirely undeveloped. F1 generates significant licensing revenue; SailGP has not built this out. As team brands strengthen (Australia with Reynolds/Jackman, Germany with Vettel), licensing becomes addressable.

**"Uncharted" as audience funnel:** If the docuseries is successful, it creates a casual-fan-to-event-attendee funnel that has been proven in F1 (Drive to Survive → US Grand Prix demand). This is the highest-leverage marketing investment.

**Sustainability premium:** As ESG-linked sponsorship grows, SailGP's Impact League positioning could command a premium from corporate sponsors with sustainability mandates. This is an emerging revenue category not yet fully priced in.

**Digital/gaming:** Sailing simulation games, fantasy leagues, and digital content are underdeveloped. Formula E has experimented with gaming integrations; SailGP has not yet built a significant gaming presence.

## Open Questions

- Which new markets are being actively pursued for team entry? What is the status of China, India, Japan, Korea, Mexico?
- What is the economic model for new franchise entry — what do new owners pay, and what do they get?
- Is there an internal roadmap to 20+ events? What are the binding constraints (venue capacity, broadcast contracts, team logistics)?
