## Strengths

- Quality of capital is exceptional and diverse. The owner roster spans institutional PE (Ares Management, Avenue Sports Fund/Marc Lasry), sovereign wealth (Mubadala for Brazil), celebrity (Ryan Reynolds, Hugh Jackman, Anne Hathaway, Kylian Mbappé, Sebastian Vettel), family office (Doug DeVos/American Magic, Idan Ofer/Quantum Pacific, Torbjörn Törnqvist), and sporting institution (Ben Ainslie group for GBR).
- Celebrity ownership is a deliberate strategy for media attention and social-media amplification, not accidental. Issa Rae, Deontay Wilder, and Jozy Altidore joining the US team alongside Marc Lasry signals that the league is actively recruiting culturally resonant names.
- Team valuation trajectory is steep. The US team sold for ~$35M in 2023; the Denmark team sold for $60M+ in February 2026 — approximately a 70% appreciation benchmark in roughly three years for comparable assets.
- By 2026, all 13 teams have private owners (or are in the process of being sold), removing the league-owned-team model that suppressed commercial incentive at the team level.
- The Ares Management stake in France (June 2025) brings institutional sports-investment expertise and signals that sophisticated PE sees a mark-to-market opportunity.

## Weaknesses / Risks

- Heavy reliance on Ellison capital historically. The league was effectively funded by Larry Ellison through the early seasons. The transition from Ellison-backed to self-sustaining is incomplete and opaque.
- Celebrity-driven valuations may be structurally fragile. If Reynolds/Jackman sell or disengage from Australia, does the team valuation hold? Comparable situation in other leagues (e.g., celebrity NBA partial ownership) suggests celebrity premiums are real but volatile.
- Team-level concentration risk: if one major owner (e.g., Mubadala, Ares) exits or a celebrity disengages publicly, it could signal weakness that cascades to other valuations.
- The league's own balance sheet and ownership structure remain opaque. Who owns SailGP the entity (beyond Coutts/Ellison)? Has any outside capital come in at the league level?

## Open Questions

- What is the long-term role of Larry Ellison — funder, board member, passive owner, eventual exit? Is there a plan for a league-level liquidity event (IPO, strategic sale, PE round at the league level)?
- What percentage of the league is owned by Ellison vs. Coutts vs. others? Has any third-party capital entered at the league (not team) level?
- How are team valuations formally determined — is there a third-party appraisal, or is it negotiated transaction-by-transaction?
- If a celebrity owner sells or disengages, what happens to their team's commercial position? Is there a right-of-first-refusal or approval mechanism?
