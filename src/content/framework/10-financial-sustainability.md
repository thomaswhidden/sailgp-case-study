## Strengths

- **$200M+ league revenue in Season 5** (2024-25), reported by Sportico
- **$230M regional economic impact** generated across host cities — strong host-city negotiating position
- **3-4 teams hitting break-even** this season per Thompson (Aug 2025) — the franchise model has proven viable for top operators
- **$8-9M top-team annual revenue** with $10M cost cap means top teams are within striking distance of operating profitability
- **Cost cap structure** prevents the runaway-spending dynamics that have damaged America's Cup teams (£150M per cycle)
- **Centralized boat manufacturing** ($5M/boat) eliminates the largest historical sailing-team cost from team P&Ls
- **$50M franchise floor** today vs. cost-covering investments in 2018 — strong franchise value appreciation curve
- **Diversified revenue streams emerging:** sponsorship (primary), broadcast share (50% of league deals to teams), hospitality, merchandise, ticketing (£60-£400+ price ladder), licensing in development
- **Bet365 already taking SailGP odds** — early validation of betting as future revenue stream

## Weaknesses & Risks

- **Most revenue streams (broadcast, licensing, betting) still undeveloped** — the league is heavily sponsorship-dependent at the team level
- **League-level P&L not public** — unclear what proportion of central operations costs are still underwritten by Ellison
- **Top team revenue ($8-9M) is 1/30th to 1/50th of F1 team revenue** — the F1 comparison implies enormous scale-up still required
- **Cost cap currently includes marketing**, which Thompson acknowledged is restraining commercially desirable spending
- **Broadcast revenue conversion** is the explicit next-5-year challenge — Thompson framed broadcast deals as binary (drive subscribers → rights fees, or no rights fees)
- **Most-watched sailing race on CBS in 40 years was 1.8M viewers** — a strong sailing audience but small in the broader US sports landscape

## Open Questions for Russell

1. At the league level, has SailGP reached operating breakeven? If not, what's the timeline?
2. What proportion of league operating costs are still being underwritten by Ellison vs. covered by team fees, sponsorship, broadcast, and event revenue?
3. What's the realistic top-line revenue ceiling for a SailGP team at maturity? $15M? $25M? $50M+?
4. As broadcast deals mature, what does the revenue mix look like at maturity?
5. What's the path from $200M league revenue today to a level that supports a multi-billion-dollar league valuation?
