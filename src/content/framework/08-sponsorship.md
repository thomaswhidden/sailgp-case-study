## Strengths

- Rolex is the title sponsor with a reported 10-year deal — a landmark anchor partnership that signals the luxury tier SailGP is positioning within. Rolex's association with sailing (Rolex Sydney-Hobart, Rolex Fastnet) makes the partnership credible, not just transactional.
- Emirates signed a 5-year global airline partnership in November 2024. Emirates's portfolio of sports sponsorships (Arsenal, Real Madrid, F1 teams) makes this a meaningful validation.
- DP World (logistics), KPMG (professional services), Accor (hotels) are multi-season league-level sponsors, suggesting stable commercial relationships rather than one-off event buys.
- Some teams are reportedly generating $10–12M in annual sponsorship revenue from their own commercial programs, suggesting the team-level commercial model is viable.
- The celebrity and PE ownership base is itself a commercial asset — Reynolds/Jackman ownership of Australia is likely worth more in earned media than any individual sponsor deal.
- BONDS clothing as Australia's title sponsor, Red Bull for Italy, Deutsche Bank for Germany — the team-level sponsorship roster is credible.

## Weaknesses / Risks

- Sponsorship revenue total and breakdown (league vs. team level) is not public. The league's financial statements have not been disclosed.
- Renewal and churn data is unknown. Are sponsors renewing? Is there attrition that the public record doesn't show?
- The league's heavy luxury-brand positioning (Rolex, Emirates, Accor) may limit addressable sponsor pool — mid-market consumer brands may not see the fit.
- Team-level sponsorship is unevenly distributed. Germany reportedly lacks sponsors; other smaller-market teams may struggle to reach $10M+ levels.
- The Impact League creates sustainability-linked sponsorship opportunities, but it is unclear whether sponsors are paying a premium for the ESG association.

## Open Questions

- What is the breakdown of total sponsorship revenue between the league and individual teams?
- What is the Rolex deal value, and does it include title naming rights for the league itself?
- What is the sponsor renewal rate? Has any major sponsor churned?
- What is the commercial model for the Impact League — does it attract its own distinct sponsor pool?
- Which teams are at $10M+ annual sponsorship, and which are struggling commercially? Is there a mechanism for the league to support financially weak teams?
