## The Sport Before SailGP

- **The America's Cup framework.** Sailing's premier event has been governed since 1851 by the Deed of Gift, ruled by the New York Supreme Court. Whoever wins selects the next venue, the next class of boat, and — critically — takes the commercial rights for the next cycle.

- **The structural commercial problem.** Andrew Thompson (SailGP MD, August 2025) recalled that as rights holders for the America's Cup between 2010 and 2017, his team faced repeated requests from broadcasters and sponsors for multi-cycle agreements that they could not honor. "We'd say, well, actually, sorry guys, we can't do that."

- **Sailing's broader fragmentation.** Across formats, classes, and disciplines, sailing had "never had an elite series that existed for the long term" (Thompson). World Sailing governed a sport more like an Olympics-of-disciplines than a sport with a coherent commercial property at the top.

- **The TV problem.** Traditional sailing was hard to watch — "white triangles on a blue background," as Coutts has framed it. Boats were slow, races were long, courses were offshore, and the visual product was inaccessible to non-sailors.

## The Founding Thesis

- **Founders.** Larry Ellison (Oracle co-founder, two-time America's Cup winner with Oracle Team USA) and Russell Coutts (five-time America's Cup winner, Olympic gold medalist in the Finn class). Both were veterans of the America's Cup commercial machinery from 2010–2017.

- **Founding mandate.** Per Thompson: "He [Ellison] is very clear from day one this needs to be run like a business. This isn't some philanthropic venture." The objective from inception was a commercially sustainable sailing league — never been done before in the sport.

- **Year founded.** October 2018.

- **Inaugural season.** 2019 — 6 league-owned national teams, 5 events. Australia (Tom Slingsby) won the inaugural championship.

## The Initial Design Choices

- **National teams, not arbitrary brands.** Each team represents a country. Per Thompson, this provides (1) built-in fan base in each country, (2) talent pool from each nation's sailing infrastructure, and (3) owner motivation to drive in-country PR and media. Selection criteria: nations must have sailors who can compete at this level AND an established sailing fan base.

- **One-design platform.** All teams race identical F50 foiling catamarans. Per Thompson, even with identical boats, the gap between top and bottom teams in a 15-minute race is approximately 4 minutes. "If teams had different equipment, you'd know the winner before you started."

- **Foiling technology.** F50s travel 3× faster than the wind, hitting nearly 60 mph. Hard wing sails, hydrofoils that lift the hull entirely from the water, eliminating drag.

- **Boat lineage.** F50 is a modification of the AC50 used in the 2017 America's Cup, itself a modification of the 2013 AC boats. Continuously evolved — a new foiling system was introduced in 2025.

- **Centralized manufacturing.** All boats produced at SailGP's Southampton facility (now part of the global HQ opened July 2025).

- **Open data sharing.** Per Thompson, SailGP shares all team data across all teams. Lower-ranked teams can see the settings the top teams use. Deliberate design choice to close performance gaps and accelerate the move from "feel-based" to data-driven sailing.

- **Stadium racing.** Short courses, harbor venues, races approximately 15 minutes long. Iconic visual product (Sydney Harbour, San Francisco Bay, Auckland, etc.).

## The Era of League-Owned Teams (2019–2022)

- **Original 2018 plan.** Per Thompson: "We had a group of owners in the room thinking we'll all kind of put money in a pot and come by, we'll all kind of work together." Original plan was a fully franchised system from day one with shared owner contributions.

- **What actually happened.** Running by committee was too slow. Ellison didn't operate that way. Ellison personally absorbed cost and capitalized the league. Plan: build value over five years, then sell teams to private owners.

- **Initial competitive dynamic.** Australia (Tom Slingsby) won the first three championships in dominant fashion. Skill differential was visible — league-controlled teams created uneven competitive depth.

- **COVID disruption.** Season 2 (2020) heavily disrupted by pandemic; abbreviated calendar.

- **Initial partnerships.** Rolex emerged as title partner; broadcast deals signed in core markets. Per Thompson, "back in 2019, 2020, you go into a room and people are like 'what the hell is SailGP, what are you even talking about.'"

## The Transition to Privatization (2023–Present)

- **First major transaction.** March 2023 — Marc Lasry / Avenue Sports Fund acquired the US team for approximately $35M. Group included Issa Rae, Deontay Wilder, Jozy Altidore. Largest team transaction in league history at that point.

- **Franchise valuation curve.** Per Thompson (Aug 2025): early days — cost-covering investments; ~Season 2–3 — $20M floor; today — $50M minimum; latest record (Doug DeVos / American Magic acquisition of Denmark, Feb 2026) reportedly $60M+.

- **Drivers of valuation growth.** (1) league recognition and brand growth; (2) scarcity — only so many teams will ever exist; (3) commercial model now works (3–4 teams hitting break-even per Thompson); (4) national-team rights are uniquely valuable in sport.
