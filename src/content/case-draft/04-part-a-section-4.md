## The Comparable Leagues Landscape

- **Formula 1 — the explicit aspirational benchmark.** Founded 1950; Liberty Media; 10 teams; ~$3.6B revenue 2024; 24 races. Drive to Survive transformation story. Per-race economics: $200–400M vs. SailGP's $26M.

- **LIV Golf.** Founded 2022; PIF/Saudi Arabia; 13 teams; 14 events. Counter-example for sovereign-funded league dynamics; broadcast struggles.

- **IPL.** Founded 2008; BCCI; 10 teams; massive media rights. Emerging-market league success; PE entry case.

- **UFC / TKO Group.** Founded 1993; Endeavor/TKO. Niche-to-mainstream playbook.

- **America's Cup.** 1851. The legacy model SailGP disrupts. Understanding why the Cup's model failed commercially is core to SailGP's origin story.

- **Extreme E / E1 Series.** Direct competitors for tech-forward, sustainability-themed, global-venue positioning.

## Sailing's Fragmentation Problem

- **World Sailing.** Governing body for sailing. Per Thompson, SailGP has a strong relationship and is a "special event of World Sailing" with its endorsement.

- **Olympic sailing's engagement challenge.** Per Thompson: "it's really hard for the casual sports fan to engage with." Many disciplines, many classes, complex rules, slow boats, inaccessible visual product.

- **The class fragmentation.** Sailing has many disciplines (Olympic classes, foiling, offshore, dinghy, keel-boat, etc.) governed under one body — unlike most sports where the world championship has a single visible format.

- **SailGP's positioning.** "The simple version of sailing, sitting at the elite level" (Thompson).

## Risk and Externalities

- **Safety incidents.** F50s race at 60+ mph. Notable incident: New Zealand-France collision in Auckland 2026 sidelined both teams. Christchurch Grand Prix 2024 cancelled mid-event due to Hector's dolphins — Coutts publicly criticized "minority interests."

- **Geopolitical exposure.** Race calendar includes Dubai, Abu Dhabi; planned expansion to China. Mirrors the LIV-PIF / F1-Saudi political concerns.

- **Sustainability positioning vs. operational footprint.** SailGP markets itself as climate-forward via the Impact League. Vulnerable to greenwashing critique given global flying of teams and equipment.

- **Single-supplier F50 manufacturing risk.** All boats produced in Southampton. What happens if there's a manufacturing disruption?

- **Talent pool depth.** Real ceiling on the global pool of F50-capable crew members.

- **Calendar/venue risk.** Venues have been moved (St-Tropez → Geneva for 2026).

## Economic Contribution

- **Reported regional economic impact.** $230M across host cities in Season 5 — approximately $26M average per event.

- **Tourism effect.** Iconic harbour venues attract international visitors. Auckland and Sydney events function as regional sports tourism draws.

- **Sponsor activation.** International partners use SailGP venues as activation platforms in markets where they want commercial presence.
