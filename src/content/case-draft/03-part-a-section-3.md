## The Audience Build

- **Two five-year arcs (Thompson, Aug 2025).** First five years (2018–2023): build product, build fan base, get eyeballs. Next five years (2023–2028): convert that audience into commercial broadcast revenue.

- **Broadcast partners (current).** UK — TNT; US — CBS Sports (live racing) and CBS national network (highlights); Canada — TSN; New Zealand — Discovery (free-to-air); Australia — Fox Sports.

- **Best US viewership data points.** 1.78M CBS viewers for the Spain race (Nov 2023) — most-watched US sailing broadcast since 1992; 1.8M average highlight viewers in 2024–25 — reportedly the highest sailing audience on CBS in 40 years; 3.47M US viewers for Race to Abu Dhabi (Nov 2025) — most-watched sailing race in US history.

- **TikTok and YouTube acceleration.** Per Thompson, the channels driving the most measurable audience growth in 2024–25. One viral clip (NZ team member dangling off the boat) hit approximately 50M views.

- **Audience composition.** Two cohorts — (1) the traditional 45–55 affluent sailing demographic; (2) a new TikTok/YouTube audience drawn to extreme sports, crashes, and speed.

- **"Racing fan" positioning, not "sailing fan."** Per Thompson, target persona is racing fans — with F1 at the top of that category and SailGP positioning in the second tier of racing properties.

## Content Strategy and the "Drive to Survive" Question

- **Existing content assets.** "Racing on the Edge" — in-house behind-the-scenes series, funded with Rolex, distributed via broadcast partners and YouTube. SailGP YouTube channel did approximately 2.1M views in Season 4.

- **"Uncharted" docuseries (launched July 2025).** Apple TV+ and multi-broadcaster distribution. Confirmed in the Thompson interview before release.

- **Thompson's skepticism of the Drive to Survive playbook.** "The market is moving on a little bit from that now... what's the next thing for content?"

- **The athlete-as-hero challenge.** Per Thompson: "If I had a pound for every marketing person who said storylines, athletes as heroes, I'd be a very rich man." Sailors aren't household names yet — Ben Ainslie in the UK, Pete Burling in Auckland, but no one with truly global recognition.

## Commercial Engine

- **Title sponsorship anchor.** Rolex — 10-year deal.

- **Major partners.** Emirates (5-year global airline partnership signed Nov 2024), DP World (logistics, multi-year), KPMG, Accor (which connects to the Mbappé deal via Accor CEO Sebastian Bazin), Mubadala (Brazil partnership and broader brand association), Deutsche Bank (Germany team presenting partner).

- **Team-level sponsorship.** Per Thompson, sponsorship is the dominant team revenue stream. Some teams reportedly clear $10–12M in annual sponsorship revenue.

- **Hospitality and ticketing growth.** Per Thompson, ticketing has grown faster than expected. Portsmouth grandstand: £60. Premium hospitality (FNB included, "apres-sail vibe"): £300–400. Sponsor zones above that.

- **Betting and gaming as emerging revenue.** Per Thompson, SailGP has commissioned studies showing the sport "lends itself well" to betting because the one-design format creates a level playing field similar to a horse racing handicap. Internal odds-generation team. Bet365 has already taken SailGP odds as a live product.

## Format Evolution

- **Current format.** 4 races in 1.5 hours of broadcast, with approximately 10-minute breaks between races where viewership measurably drops.

- **The Group A / Group B proposal (Thompson, Aug 2025).** Two pools of 10 teams running concurrent races to enable expansion to 20 teams without lengthening the broadcast window. Allows back-to-back racing without gaps.

- **Why 20 teams.** Per Thompson, comparison to Premier League (20) and NFL (32). "You kind of look at other leagues, there's enough room for those teams to all live side by side."

- **Growth pace philosophy.** Deliberately one or two teams per year, one venue per year. Not a fire sale. May settle at 14 or 16 if that's the right number. Italy and Brazil were added Season 5; Sweden/Artemis added Season 6.
