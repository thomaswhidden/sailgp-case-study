## The Cost Cap and Central Services Model

- **The $10M team cost cap (Thompson, Aug 2025).** Covers everything: sailor salaries, accommodation, travel, training, business support, commercial, marketing, boats, logistics.

- **Why a cost cap exists.** Per Thompson: without it, design and build of boats would consume team budgets the way they do in the America's Cup, where teams spend approximately £150M per cycle. SailGP has solved this through central design and build. Next runaway-cost risk: athlete salaries, with 5–6 elite sailors becoming attractive enough to drive bidding wars.

- **Active discussion to remove marketing from the cap.** Per Thompson, league and teams are negotiating to exclude marketing/social spend from the cap so teams can invest more aggressively in fan engagement.

- **Central services model.** Teams pay two central costs to SailGP: (1) shared services (logistics, race management, umpires); (2) capital assets (the boats themselves). Set at start of year; SailGP works within that envelope.

- **Boat economics.** Each F50 costs $5M to manufacture. Each team has one boat plus a shared spares pool for the tour. Centralized in Southampton.

- **Logistics.** Approximately 140 forty-foot containers per tour. F50s detach at the bow (50ft → 40ft) to fit standard shipping containers. Disassembled within 24 hours of a race. DP World is logistics partner. SailGP chartered its own ship for the 2025–26 tour due to shipping industry conditions.

## Team Revenue and the Path to Break-Even

- **Top team revenue: $8–9M USD per season (Thompson, Aug 2025).** Against the $10M cap, this puts top teams within striking distance of operating profitability.

- **3–4 teams hitting break-even or better in Season 5.** Per Thompson — "investors look at that and think okay, well, I'm not going to have to fund a load of losses to begin with."

- **Primary team revenue source: sponsorship.** Boat branding space is "a pretty significant floating billboard" (Thompson). League retains some space for Rolex; teams own the rest.

- **Broadcast revenue distribution.** 50% of league broadcast rights revenue distributed to teams. Broadcast revenue is still small overall.

- **Other team revenue streams.** Hospitality, merchandise, in-territory event partnership.

## League-Level Economics

- **Reported league revenue (Season 5, 2024–25): $200M+.** Per Sportico coverage.

- **Reported regional economic impact: $230M across host cities (Season 5).** Approximately $26M average per Grand Prix — 4× Season 1 levels.

- **Ticketed spectators (Season 5): 112,000+.** Per league disclosures. Auckland — 25,000; Portsmouth — 20,000.

- **Auckland 2025: 70% of 20,000 ticketed spectators had never touched a sailing boat (Thompson).** SailGP is recruiting non-sailors, not converting sailors.

- **Net Promoter Score: +58 average.** Per league disclosures.

- **Title partnership: Rolex 10-year deal.** Other major partners: Emirates, DP World, KPMG, Accor, Mubadala, Deutsche Bank.

## The Athlete Layer

- **"Best of the best" positioning.** Per Thompson: "We've got the best athletes in our sport competing in CGP. There's no B squad."

- **The 6-athlete crew, 3-of-which-must-be-from-affiliated-nation rule.** Reduced from 5-of-6 a year before the Thompson interview, opening the transfer market.

- **First-ever sailing transfer fee (2025).** Giles Scott from Britain to Canada. Scott took ownership equity in the Canadian team in addition to the playing role. Transfer fee counts toward the $10M cap.

- **Long-term contracts emerging.** Italian team has signed 5-year deals — athlete career certainty independent of America's Cup cycles.

- **Athlete equity model.** Athletes receive equity in teams as part of compensation. Examples: Ben Ainslie sweat equity in Britain; Jimmy Spithill co-ownership and CEO of Italy.

- **Salary range.** Thompson declined specifics; said "somewhere in the middle" of low-hundreds-of-thousands to high millions.

- **Talent pool concern.** Thompson estimates only 5–6 elite sailors are currently "attractive enough to drive bidding wars." Realistic global pool of F50-capable crew members appears narrow.

- **National federation pathway development.** Spanish team created a "foiling pathway" supported by the Spanish federation. Vision: Olympic pathway → SailGP pathway as elite professional destination.
